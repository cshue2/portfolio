Retrospect was created as a class project to learn more about research based UI/UX design. I was given the freedom to create any product I wanted that solved a problem. With all the time and money I spend on music streaming services, how do I learn about my historical music listening habits to 1. decide which music apps are worth my time and money and 2. How can I quickly and easily view my historical listening?

In order to answer these questions, I designed Retrospect, a fictional app that tracks and compares your historical listening habits by syncing up with your current music streaming apps. Retrospect tells you everything from which app you spend the most time in, to what songs and genres you listen to the most on a monthly and weekly basis. Using multiple data visualizations, quick and exciting interactions are made with Retrospect so you're viewing more than just data, but a fun and visual discovery about yourself and your ears.

I was also tasked with creating a user flow mapping a users journey from initial product landing page to check-out and app download.

My target user group was 20s-50s aged users who are not necessarily music junkies. My design decisions were made to increase scanability, usability, and to inform our user group how Retrospect can benefit them and save them money.
