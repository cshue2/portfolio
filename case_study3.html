HelloWallet is a financial technology company that develops a financial wellness app helping the hourly worker, family of 5, college student, executive, manage their wealth, save money, and learn how to avoid debt.

As the UI/UX design intern I worked on various projects from visualizing different tyes of 401(K)s to iterating first concepts for mobile saving and spending data visualizations that aid the budgeting portion of the app. HelloWallet was my first glimpse into the UI/UX world but also showed me the importance of designing with a purpose; designing to help the people around us. To think about users as our neighbors and help them better understand the often-confusing fiscal world.

Three of the major projects I worked on during my time at HelloWallet were:

1. Iterating the first mobile concepts for a new budgeting feature, the Save/Spend graph. This tool allows users to set a goal within a certain about of time and helps guide them on their spending in order to save. The mobile screens at the top were a collaboration with the designer working on the desk top version.
